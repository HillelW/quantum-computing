# Quantum Computing

Jupyter notebooks covering quantum computing from mathematical foundations through physical implementations.

## Notebooks

1. **Complex Numbers** — Algebra and geometry of complex numbers as the foundation for quantum mechanics
2. **Complex Vector Spaces** — Vector spaces over the complex numbers and their role in describing quantum states
3. **Classical to Quantum** — From classical bit manipulation to quantum superposition and entanglement
4. **Basic Quantum Theory** — Postulates, measurements, and the mathematical structure of quantum mechanics
5. **Architecture** — Quantum circuits, qubits, and how quantum computers are organized
6. **Algorithms** — Deutsch-Jozsa, Simon's algorithm, and other quantum speedups
7. **Programming Languages** — Quantum assembly language and higher-level quantum programming frameworks
8. **Theoretical CS** — Complexity classes from deterministic to quantum Turing machines
9. **Cryptography** — Classical cryptography, quantum key exchange (BB84, B92, EPR), and quantum teleportation
10. **Information Theory** — Shannon entropy, von Neumann entropy, data compression, and quantum error correction
11. **Hardware** — Physical implementations: ion traps, linear optics, NMR, and superconductors
12. **Grover's Algorithm** — Quantum search with complete implementation and convergence analysis
