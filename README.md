# Quantum Computing

Jupyter notebooks covering quantum computing from mathematical foundations through physical implementations.

## Notebooks

1. [**Complex Numbers**](quantum-computing/01_complex_numbers.ipynb) — Algebra and geometry of complex numbers as the foundation for quantum mechanics
2. [**Complex Vector Spaces**](quantum-computing/02_complex_vector_spaces.ipynb) — Vector spaces over the complex numbers and their role in describing quantum states
3. [**Classical to Quantum**](quantum-computing/03_classical_to_quantum.ipynb) — From classical bit manipulation to quantum superposition and entanglement
4. [**Basic Quantum Theory**](quantum-computing/04_basic_quantum_theory.ipynb) — Postulates, measurements, and the mathematical structure of quantum mechanics
5. [**Architecture**](quantum-computing/05_architecture.ipynb) — Quantum circuits, qubits, and how quantum computers are organized
6. [**Algorithms**](quantum-computing/06_algorithms.ipynb) — Deutsch-Jozsa, Simon's algorithm, and other quantum speedups
7. [**Programming Languages**](quantum-computing/07_programming_languages.ipynb) — Quantum assembly language and higher-level quantum programming frameworks
8. [**Theoretical CS**](quantum-computing/08_theoretical_cs.ipynb) — Complexity classes from deterministic to quantum Turing machines
9. [**Cryptography**](quantum-computing/09_cryptography.ipynb) — Classical cryptography, quantum key exchange (BB84, B92, EPR), and quantum teleportation
10. [**Information Theory**](quantum-computing/10_information_theory.ipynb) — Shannon entropy, von Neumann entropy, data compression, and quantum error correction
11. [**Hardware**](quantum-computing/11_hardware.ipynb) — Physical implementations: ion traps, linear optics, NMR, and superconductors
12. [**Grover's Algorithm**](quantum-computing/grovers_algorithm.ipynb) — Quantum search with complete implementation and convergence analysis
